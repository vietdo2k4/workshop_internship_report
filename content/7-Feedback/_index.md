> Here, you can freely share your personal opinions about your experience participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program. This will help the FCAJ team improve any shortcomings based on the following aspects:

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Learning Environment**  
The FCAJ community environment is dynamic and welcoming. While the program is primarily online and self-study-based rather than in-person at an office, community members are always ready to help and answer questions — even outside working hours. The positive and open learning atmosphere means I never felt alone even when studying independently most of the time.

**2. Support from Mentors / Admin Team**  
The mentors and admin team at FCAJ are consistently supportive, providing detailed guidance and clear explanations whenever I was confused. What I appreciated most is that they actively encourage everyone to ask questions, no matter how basic. The admin team was also very helpful with paperwork, documentation, and creating the right conditions for all members to learn and work smoothly.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
The tasks assigned during the internship aligned well with what I learned at university, while also introducing entirely new areas I had never explored before — such as AWS Serverless architecture, cloud system design, and real-world product development workflows. This allowed me to reinforce my foundational knowledge while gaining meaningful practical experience.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
Throughout the internship, I picked up many new skills: using project management tools, remote teamwork, technical report writing, and professional communication within a community setting. The regular FCAJ meetup events were especially valuable — listening to experienced practitioners share their journeys helped broaden my industry perspective and gave me clearer career direction.

**5. Community Culture & Team Spirit**  
The culture within FCAJ is very positive: everyone respects one another and takes their work seriously while keeping things friendly and approachable. Even though everyone works independently from different locations, whenever an issue comes up, people genuinely step in to help regardless of their role or seniority. That sense of belonging made me feel like a real member of the community, not just an intern passing through.
